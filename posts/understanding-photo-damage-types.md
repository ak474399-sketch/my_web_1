## Common Types of Photo Damage

Old photographs can suffer from many forms of degradation. Understanding the type of damage helps you choose the right restoration approach.

### 1. Fading and Color Shift

**What it looks like:** Colors appear washed out, yellowish, or have an overall color cast.

**Why it happens:** UV light exposure and chemical reactions in the photo paper cause dyes to break down over decades.

**Best tool:** [Faded Photo Repair](/restore/faded-photo-repair)

### 2. Scratches and Creases

**What it looks like:** Visible lines, marks, or fold lines across the image.

**Why it happens:** Physical handling, improper storage, or accidental damage.

**Best tool:** [Scratch Removal](/restore/scratch-removal)

### 3. Water Damage

**What it looks like:** Stains, warping, mold spots, or tide lines on the image.

**Why it happens:** Flooding, humidity, or accidental spills.

**Best tool:** [Water Damaged Photo Repair](/restore/water-damaged-photo-repair)

### 4. Blur and Softness

**What it looks like:** Overall lack of sharpness, soft edges, or motion blur.

**Why it happens:** Camera shake during exposure, focus issues, or degradation of the print.

**Best tool:** [Blurry Photo Fix](/restore/blurry-photo-fix)

### 5. Torn or Missing Sections

**What it looks like:** Parts of the photo are physically missing or torn apart.

**Why it happens:** Rough handling, accidents, or deliberate damage.

**Best tool:** [Torn Photo Repair](/restore/torn-photo-repair)

## Choosing the Right Approach

For photos with multiple types of damage, we recommend starting with the most severe issue first, then applying additional tools for refinement. Our AI handles each type differently with specialized prompts and processing techniques.
